How to Be a Great Teacher

Tips for building trust, respect, and a productive learning environment

Key Principles

  • If you make a suggestion to your students, and it gets judged, take ownership by making it clear it was your idea.
  • Stay consistent in your expectations, feedback, and behavior—it helps students feel secure and focused.
  • When assigning work, be clear and timely with all the necessary details and deadlines.
  • Avoid last-minute tasks or requirements—keep communication transparent from the beginning.
  • Act as a mentor, not an opponent. Be on their side and show that you genuinely care about their growth.
  • Demonstrate your expertise—share why you’re a reliable source and why your insights matter.
  • Always try to support your students—your role is to help them succeed, not just to grade them.
  • When giving feedback, start with something positive to create a constructive atmosphere.
  • Keep your tone motivating and encouraging—even critical feedback can be delivered with care.
  • Make sure your students feel you’re invested in their success, not just tolerating them.
  • If a disagreement arises, support your argument with realistic examples, not hypotheticals.
  • Be mindful of your tone—use authority only when it’s truly necessary, not as a default.
  • Empathize—try to understand your students’ perspectives and challenges.
  • Respect and value student input—don't impose your views as the only valid ones.
  • Be approachable and human—students are more likely to open up and learn when they feel safe.
  • Promote collaboration over competition to create a more inclusive environment.
